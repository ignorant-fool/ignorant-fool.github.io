"Spring."

That lovely few months where the air starts to get fresher (?), we stop wearing our jumpers as much (?), and wait huh? We just left Spring? Could've sworn we've just entered Winter instead, but alas, we are behoved to use the Japanese seasons when discussing our favourite animated Chinese cartoons. With the *Summer* season started, it is time to discuss our thoughts on *Spring*.

I will start with the not-currently airing anime I watched, followed by the Spring season anime in roughly worst-to-best order. Note that these are my thoughts and opinions, so don't get too mad (or do, fight me). I'll give each of the currently airing shows a one-word rating from the following, along with some brief discussion/notes.
    - dropped: I dropped it for whatever reason
    - incomplete (___): this the first cour of a multi-season show, but will give it a preliminary rating in brackets
    - average: perfectly okay, nothing spectacular
    - good: I enjoyed the show
    - great: I really enjoyed the show
    - AOTS: My choice for Anime of the Season!

Although it should be obvious, this is your only spoiler warning!

---
Not-currently airing:

**One Piece** (715-815)

I am a pretty big fan of One Piece - definitely one of my favourite manga of all time (debatable whether it is my actual favourite, but that's for another post). In mid-2020 I decided in the lockdown-era to start watching the anime from beginning to end, including every filler and film. I figured 929 episodes (at the time) shouldn't take too long, a year at max?

HAHAHA

Three-and-a-bit years later, here we are. Everyone on the internet was right - Dressrosa was a draaaaaaaag to get through, it was drawn out and for no good reason. Whole Cake Island though? This shit slaps. The Toei team, and Oda too, just had so much fun with this arc. The songs, the characters, it's just great. In retrospect, definitely a great arc.


Also WOW, 100 episodes in a season! Almost one per day! *if only I'd kept that up the entire time, I'd be done by now*

**Kimi ni Todoke** (1-10)

As a certified weeb, I've recently decided to try and learn to read/speak/understand 日本語 (Japanese). It's going...  not terribly? I definitely need to hit the grammar books more but kanji and vocabulary are about on track for where I want to be, but enough on that as I'm sure I'll make another post for that in due time.
For now, as part of the whole *immersion dealio*, I've opted to start watching an anime using Japanese subs, and that anime is Kimi ni Todoke! I've read a fair amount of the manga so figured this would make a good choice.
Boy was I wrong.
It's quite difficult to keep up with, and I can't read anything as fast as they're talking, but that's okay. I'm enjoying the challenge, and looking forward to rewatching this in a few years *without* the subs.

Currently airing:

**Ao no Orchestra (Cour 1)** incomplete (average)

*Blue Orchestra* had the potential to be something pretty damn great, and I think it still has that potential sitting in the back, somewhere, deep, behind that "what the fuck why has no one licenced this show" elephant in the room. I am enjoying this quite a bit, particularly since I am an ex-band nerd from high school myself. Akine is a budget Asuka, the bassists use German bows (awesome btw). Definitely hurt by the fan subs not being musically knowledgeable, misusing (and abusing) some terms. Hopefully the second cour keeps up the good work!

**Ousama Ranking** average

*Ousama Ranking* - Side Story time. Yeah look it was okay, not much to say really. Just a bunch of SOL stories that didn't make the cut in Season 1. Hiling-sama is a beautiful milf as always, and very sad about what happens to King Desha. 

**Megami no Cafe Terrace** average

Hoo boy, I was missing my seasonal harem until this came-along. I'm up to date in the manga, and somehow been very on the fence on who best girl is. Watching the anime, I was almost led to be believed that Akane-chan, the コーヒー master herself is best girl! But who am I kidding, they're actually all best girls! Episode 11 is going to sell-out the blu-rays I tell ya'.

**Jigokuraku** average

The first of the *true* anime, silly battle shounens. Yeah not gonna lie, underwhelming. No nipples on the bad girls (bad dudes are nippled up though, huh?) ruined the immersion for me. Yuzuriha is cute, and I wish we got to see more of the oiran.

**Kuma Kuma Kuma Bear Punch!** average

KUMA. Kuma kuma *kuma*, kuma.  tl;dr she gets kanshou and bakuya (UNLIMITED BEAR WORKS) and increases her loli harem. Need I say anymore? S3 when??

**Tonikaku Kawaii S2** good

It's just so damn fluffy man I can't. This season starts to hint at more of Tsukasa's background (oh how I wonder what it could be), which is nice and all, but the highlight is BEST GIRL TOKIKO WOO. Love that gal, and I am soooo not keen for next season's climax let me tell you that.

**Kono Subarashii Sekai ni Bakuen wo!** good

Best ED of the season, Megumin and Yuyu just too kawaii. Nice to get some history on the explosion loli and her cat. Time for KonoSuba season 3 now!

**Demon Slayer - Swordsmith Village Arc** good

It's the weakest of the story arcs, and I don't think ufotable brought their A-game to hype it up like the other seasons. Good to see Mitsuri and Nezuko together tho, definitely a good pair of gooder girls.

**Dr Stone: New World** good

Francois is the goat, and I have nothing else to say about this season.

**Mahoutsukai no Yome S2** good

This is borderline great, purely because I'm a manga reader and know what's coming up. Alone, this season was a bit slow, but it's just *magic*, y'know?

**Tengoku Daimakyou** ???

I'm not normally one to think that how a season ends makes or breaks that season, but damn do I think the ending broke this. I thought this was really great, like super sugoi almost AOTS great. But that ending, it just didn't vibe with me, and I don't think I'll follow up with the manga or any potential season 2. Really hard to grade this one, as 11/13 were great, and the rest were drop-worthy.

**Vinland Saga S2 (Cour 2)** great

*Vinland Saga* Season 2, the lowest of my greats (which says a lot about the other shows). Season 2 of *Vinland Saga* was nothing like the first, but that doesn't mean it was bad at all. The "Farmland" arc was a different look at war, from the point of view of those not-at-war and those who have ceased being a part of war. Thorfinn, the tiny viking who almost rivalled best-boy Askellad, is not rejecting violence - what a glow-up. 
This second cour in particular I found better than the first, with episode 20 being very painful to watch.

**Kimi wa Houkago Insomnia** great

Is Shiromaru my best girl of the season? quite possible. She's cute. She has a cat. She runs an arcade. She's cute. She's adorable. She knows about astrophotography. Did I mention she's cute?
tfw no introvert arcade gamer cat lady tomboy gf :sigh:.

Also this show is lit, sad undertones but great highly recommend.

**Skip to Loafer** great

Is Nao-chan my best girl of the season? Probably not, but she's up there. Definitely was not expecting to get any trans representation this season, so it was nice to see that it was handled well here. 
Also I "skip" Mitsumi and Shima so hard.



---

Which means... my AOTS for Spring 2023 is...

**Oshi no Ko** AND **Yamada-kun to Lv999 no Koi wo Suru** 

I know, I know, but I couldn't pick between the two.

*Oshi no Ko* got a fucking *film* for episode 1, and it was legitimately the only way to do it, so very good on them for doing it. I'm a massive fan of Aka and Mengo's work, and Doga Kobo smashed this one OUT OF THE PARK. I cannot wait for season 2 of this modern classic.

*Yamada-kun*, on the other hand, took me a few episodes to get in the rhythm. I'm a manga reader and up to date, but damn at first I thought it was a bit janky. After I got over the visuals, I realised 
1. the visuals are great, and
2. who cares because it's heckin' cute.

From very early in the season, this has been lowkey AOTS for me. Very very hopeful for a future season 2!
